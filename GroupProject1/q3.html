Evoulution of the Internet

The internet's evolution has been marked by significant advancements in technology and infrastructure:

1990s:
Widespread adoption of commercial internet services and rapid expansion of online content
Late 1990's
The "Dot-com bubble" refers to a period in the late 1990s and early 2000s characterized by excessive speculation and surge in internet-related businesses. Despite the fact that many of these businesses were losing money, investors increased their values because they anticipated significant gains.
2000s
Introduction of broadband connections, enabling faster and more reliable data transfer
Mobile Internet
The widespread adoption of smartphones and establishment wireless connectivity, leading to anytime, anywhere access. These devices feature more than the traditional call only features of olden phones which all revolutionized the modern world. From communication to working.
internet Of things
This connectivity improves efficiency and convenience by enabling data collection and sharing from objects like appliances, automobiles, and devices. IoT makes it possible to remotely monitor, manage, and analyse linked items, which helps create smarter homes, cities, and businesses.
